# Maurice Blackburn Brand History

Sourced 2026-07-11 from: MB's own "About us" page (mauriceblackburn.com.au), Wikipedia's "Maurice Blackburn (law firm)" article, Wikipedia's biography of Maurice Blackburn the person, and general web search — cross-checked across all four with no contradictions.

## Founding

The firm was founded in **1919** as **"Maurice Blackburn & Co."** by **Maurice Blackburn** (19 November 1880 – 31 March 1944), an Australian lawyer and politician. He studied arts and law at the University of Melbourne (graduated 1909), began practising law in 1910, and was active in the Victorian Socialist Party from the same year. His firm focused from the outset on trade union law and civil liberties.

Blackburn was also a career politician: Victorian Legislative Assembly member for Essendon (1914–1917), Fitzroy (1925–1927), and Clifton Hill (1927–1934), becoming Speaker of the Victorian Legislative Assembly in 1933; later federal MP for Bourke (1934–1943). He clashed repeatedly with Labor leadership over conscription and socialist positions, resulting in two expulsions and reinstatements from the party. His widow, Doris Blackburn (née Hordern), won his former federal seat as an Independent Labor candidate in 1946.

## Name evolution

The firm operated as **Maurice Blackburn & Co**, then **Maurice Blackburn Cashman**, before adopting its current name, **Maurice Blackburn Lawyers**.

## Key milestones and notable cases

- **The 40-Hour Working Week Case** — with the ACTU, helped establish the 40-hour working week.
- Cases establishing **equal pay for women and Indigenous workers**.
- **1998 MUA waterfront dispute** — represented maritime union workers.
- **GIO Class Action** — the first shareholder class action win in Australian legal history.
- **Centro Class Action** — the biggest shareholder class action settlement in Australian legal history at the time.
- **Black Saturday bushfire class actions** — totalling roughly $794 million in combined settlements; MB's own materials describe this as the largest class action settlement in Australian history.
- **BRCA1 gene patent case** — established that a mutated cancer-causing gene cannot be patented under Australian law.
- **Mohamed Haneef terrorism case (2010)** and a subsequent defamation matter.
- Since starting its class actions practice in 1998, MB has recovered more than **$5 billion**, and its practice is responsible for more than two-thirds of all class action recoveries in the first 25 years of Australia's class action regime.
